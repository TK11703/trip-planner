# Copy and Content Contract: Branding Refresh

This contract defines user-facing language expectations for the refresh.

## Tone

- Modern, helpful, calm, and practical.
- Focused on successful trip planning, not technology or infrastructure.
- Clear enough for travelers planning by car, train, plane, boat, or a mix of modes.
- Encouraging without sounding like a travel marketplace advertisement.

## Replace outdated language

- Replace old product-voice uses of "Journey", "Journeys", "Explorer", "Explore", "expedition", "trailhead", and compass/scout metaphors where they appear as brand language.
- Do not rewrite historical user-entered trip titles, notes, event names, or shared content.
- Do not remove ordinary uses of words when they are part of user data or external content outside the app's authored UI.

## Preferred language

- Use terms such as trip, route, itinerary, plan, timeline, schedule, leg, stop, stay, reservation, activity, details, checklist, reminder, and tip.
- Use action labels such as "Plan a trip", "Review recent trips", "Add a stop", "Add trip details", "Check your timeline", and "Keep planning".
- Use practical planning tips such as leaving transfer buffers, grouping reservations by day, checking departure times, saving confirmation details, and reviewing weather or opening hours before departure.

## Disallowed user-facing content

- Do not describe frameworks, databases, identity providers, hosting platforms, cloud services, or internal architecture in public or core planning copy.
- Do not use copy that implies the app books hotels, restaurants, transport tickets, or public listings unless that capability exists.
- Do not use vehicle references in a way that implies live route planning, fare lookup, or ticket purchase.

## Surface coverage

- Home page hero and cards.
- Navigation and account menu labels when brand-related.
- Recent trips headings and empty states.
- Trip list, trip detail, timeline, event detail, sharing, notifications, profile/account, and public informational surfaces where old brand language appears.
- Status, loading, validation, denied, and unavailable messages where copy currently uses old metaphors or technology-focused language.
